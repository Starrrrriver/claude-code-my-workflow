# Reviewer Response Templates

This file contains two templates used by the `/reviewer-response` skill:
- **Part A:** Markdown draft structure (`response_draft.md`)
- **Part B:** LaTeX letter structure (`response_letter.tex`)

---

## Part B — LaTeX Letter Template

```latex
%% response_letter.tex
%% Response to Reviewers — LaTeX version
%% Generated by /reviewer-response skill
%% Compile with: pdflatex response_letter.tex

\documentclass[12pt,letterpaper]{letter}
% For APS journals (PRL, PRD): consider \documentclass[aps,prl,12pt]{revtex4-2}

\usepackage{amsmath,amssymb}
\usepackage{xcolor}
\usepackage{hyperref}
\usepackage{enumitem}
\usepackage{geometry}
\usepackage{booktabs}
\usepackage{longtable}
\geometry{margin=1in}

%% -------------------------------------------------------
%% Custom commands for reviewer responses
%% -------------------------------------------------------

%% Quote a reviewer's verbatim comment in italic block
\newcommand{\reviewerquote}[1]{%
  \begin{quote}
    \itshape #1
  \end{quote}%
}

%% Describe a manuscript change with location, description, new text
%% Usage: \change{Section 3, p.~4, l.~12}{Added sentence}{New text here}
\newcommand{\change}[3]{%
  \noindent\textbf{Location:} #1\\
  \textbf{Change:} #2\\
  \textbf{New text:} \textit{``#3''}%
}

%% Color for reviewer point labels
\definecolor{reviewercolor}{RGB}{0,70,127}

%% Reviewer point label
\newcommand{\reviewerpoint}[1]{%
  \vspace{1em}
  \noindent{\color{reviewercolor}\textbf{#1}}%
  \vspace{0.3em}%
}

%% -------------------------------------------------------
%% Document
%% -------------------------------------------------------

\begin{document}

\begin{letter}{The Editorial Board\\
  \textit{[Journal Name]}\\
  [Journal Address]}

\opening{Dear Editor,}

We thank you and the referees for the careful reading of our manuscript
\textit{``[Paper Title]''} (Manuscript~\#~[Manuscript Number])
and for the constructive comments.
We have revised the manuscript accordingly and address each point in detail below.

All page and line numbers refer to the \textbf{revised} manuscript.
Changes are indicated in \textbf{bold} in the revised version.

%% -------------------------------------------------------
\section*{Summary of Major Changes}
%% -------------------------------------------------------

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em]
  \item [Change 1: brief description]
  \item [Change 2: brief description]
  \item [Change 3: brief description]
\end{itemize}

%% -------------------------------------------------------
\section*{Response to Reviewer~1}
%% -------------------------------------------------------

\subsection*{Point R1.1: [Paraphrased concern]}

\reviewerpoint{Reviewer's comment:}
\reviewerquote{[Paste verbatim reviewer text here]}

\noindent\textbf{Our response:}
[Substantive response text here.]

\change{Section~X, p.~Y, l.~Z}{[Description of change]}{[New or modified text]}

%% ---

\subsection*{Point R1.2: [Paraphrased concern]}

\reviewerpoint{Reviewer's comment:}
\reviewerquote{[Verbatim text]}

\noindent\textbf{Our response:}
[...]

\change{[Location]}{[Change]}{[New text]}

%% -------------------------------------------------------
\section*{Response to Reviewer~2}
%% -------------------------------------------------------

\subsection*{Point R2.1: [Paraphrased concern]}

\reviewerpoint{Reviewer's comment:}
\reviewerquote{[Verbatim text]}

\noindent\textbf{Our response:}
[...]

\change{[Location]}{[Change]}{[New text]}

%% -------------------------------------------------------
\section*{Complete List of Changes}
%% -------------------------------------------------------

\begin{longtable}{lp{6cm}p{4cm}l}
  \toprule
  \# & Location & Change & Triggered by \\
  \midrule
  1 & Section~X, p.~Y & [Description] & R[N].[M] \\
  2 & Eq.~([N]) & [Description] & R[N].[M] \\
  3 & Table~[N] & [Description] & R[N].[M] \\
  \bottomrule
\end{longtable}

\closing{We hope that the revised manuscript meets the standards of \textit{[Journal Name]}
and look forward to hearing from you.}

\end{letter}
\end{document}
```

## Usage Notes

### Markdown draft (`response_draft.md`)
- Generated automatically by `/reviewer-response` skill (Step 4)
- Placeholder values in `[brackets]` must be filled in by the author
- Page/line references require author input after revision
- The skill pre-fills reviewer quotes from parsed comment files

### LaTeX letter (`response_letter.tex`)
- Generated on request (Step 6) using `pdflatex`
- For **PRL/PRD** (APS journals): change `\documentclass` to `revtex4-2` with `aps,prl` options
- For **JHEP/PLB/EPJC** (SISSA/Springer): standard `letter` or `article` class works fine
- The `\reviewerquote` macro renders reviewer text in italic block quote
- The `\change` macro structures each manuscript modification uniformly
- Compile twice for correct cross-references: `pdflatex response_letter.tex && pdflatex response_letter.tex`
